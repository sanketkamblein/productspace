Problem Statement

Hospital admission during COVID pandemic: the usual journey — from symptoms → call doctor → test → get reports → find hospital → follow health officials → check dashboard → check cost of bed → wait for admission

COVID-19 patients and caretakers are unable to access information that is crucial and specific to their needs. Information is fragmented across multiple sources, difficult to verify, and rapidly changing — creating confusion, anxiety, and poor decision-making during a health crisis.

Analysis — Prioritization of Requirements

Prioritization of requirements — ranked by Demand/Wishes and Priority. Top requirements: real-time availability of hospital beds, platform to share critical health data, digital patient records, scalable data visualization, separate dashboards for patients and healthcare officials.
Problems identified — 4 key gaps: Beds/Hospitals availability · Plasma donors · Data visualization · Asymptomatic patient needs
Problem 01

Beds / Hospitals Availability

Data isn't visible for available hospitals / wards and the beds for admission at one place.

Problem 02

Plasma Donors

No consolidated place where people can request or show availability to donate blood and plasma cells.

Problem 03

Data Visualization

Need for a dashboard with data analysis where time is saved for data comprehension.

Problem 04

Asymptomatic Patients

Specific needs of asymptomatic COVID patients remain unaddressed by existing platforms.

Re-analysis — Re-define · Re-design · Re-evaluate

Learnings and Insights — 4 core problems identified from validation round
Problem re-defined: COVID-19 patients and caretakers unable to access information crucial and specific to each of them. The problem centered around symptomatic COVID patients increasing who needed hospital admission while hospitals expanded their beds in response.
Problem A

Lack of Consolidation (where to find it?)

Crucial information in different places. Who: Symptomatic patients (A1), home quarantined (A3), caretakers (A5), potential patients (A6).

Problem B

Lack of Reliability (should I trust it?)

Hard to determine whether a piece of information comes from a credible source. Who: Asymptomatic patients (A2) mainly, all A1–A6.

Problem C

Lack of Scalability (should I trust it?)

Things change quickly — information may be outdated and no longer reliable. Who: Asymptomatic patients (A2) mainly, all A1–A6.

Problem D

Lack of Clarity (what does it mean?)

Guidelines, timelines, procedures can be misleading, confusing or contradictory. Who: Positive tested patients (A1, A2), caretakers (A5).

Learnings & Insights — IMPACT

Learnings & Insights — IMPACT (Solvency model)
IMPACT: 10 lakh people are getting tested every day. Out of 41 interviewees, 22 faced an issue with misinformation and misconception.
Stakeholders
  • Suspected Patients

Our Offering
  • Consolidated information in one place
  • Fact-checked updates on latest development
  • Facilitating appropriate consultation
Impact
  • Reduced misinformation, confusion and anxiety
  • Better decision-making
  • Everyone is on the same page

Consequences of NOT solving the problem

  • People will have to use multiple information sources before making a decision
  • They will consult multiple doctors before deciding a treatment
  • Wrong decision based on non-accurate information
  • Increased spread due to misconception

The Prototype

Web app: consolidated information from verified resources, identifies fake news, multi-language support, services nearby (COVID centres, hospitals, fever clinics, toll-free, BBMP), doctors nearby, map view. Mobile interface: notification-first app giving updates on new guidelines with real-time timestamped UPDATE feed.

Web App — Consolidated Information from Verified Resources

The website gives consolidated information from verified resources and helps identify fake news. Features: multi-language support, services nearby (COVID centres, hospitals, fever clinics, toll-free, BBMP), doctors nearby, map view, and real-time updates feed.

Mobile Interface — Notification-first

The application requires a mobile interface over web. The app gives notifications of new updates and required information for guidelines — with a real-time UPDATE feed showing timestamped, source-attributed COVID news and policy changes.
